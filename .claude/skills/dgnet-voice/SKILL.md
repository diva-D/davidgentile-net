# How writing for davidgentile.net should sound

**Read `/AUDIENCE.md` first.** Voice rules are the mechanics; the audience doc is the target. If a sentence obeys every rule here but the reader described in `AUDIENCE.md` would skim past it, the sentence still fails.

Most AI prose is grammatically perfect and emotionally dead. You can spot it from across the room. The reader's pattern-matcher fires before they finish the first sentence and they bounce. This skill exists to keep that from happening on davidgentile.net.

The bar is simple: if a sentence sounds like it was generated, rewrite it. Quirks over polish. Specifics over abstractions. Short over long. Never reach for a fancier word when a plain one does the job.

## Punctuation rules (non-negotiable)

**No em dashes.** Anywhere. Not in post bodies, not in narrations, not in raw item text inside artefacts, not in descriptions, not in social drafts. This is the single strongest AI tell in modern prose. Use one of these instead, depending on what the dash was actually doing:

- A pause inside a sentence -> comma, semicolon, or full stop
- An aside or interruption -> parentheses, or split into two sentences
- A connector for a definition -> colon, "is", or just rephrase
- A range -> en dash (–) or hyphen
- Authentic informal text (chat, voice memo) -> ellipsis (...), comma, or hyphen-hyphen (--)

**No en dashes for prose.** Same rule. Reserve en dashes for numeric ranges only (e.g. "10–15 minutes").

**Sparing italics, sparing bold.** AI overreaches both. One italicised phrase per paragraph is plenty. Bold is for actual emphasis you'd say with your voice, not for sprinkling visual texture.

## Phrase blocklist (do not use)

Each of these is an instant AI smell. If one slips into a draft, it goes.

**Hollow descriptors:** robust, seamless, seamlessly, effortless, intricate, multifaceted, nuanced, holistic, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, essential, paramount, transformative, unprecedented, unparalleled, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art.

**Hype verbs:** delve, leverage, harness, unlock, unleash, supercharge, elevate, foster, cultivate, navigate (the complexities of), embark, embark on a journey.

**Filler abstractions:** myriad, plethora, tapestry, landscape, realm, world (as in "in today's world"), arena, ecosystem, paradigm, paradigm shift.

**Stale openers:** "In today's fast-paced world", "In an era of", "In the realm of", "When it comes to", "It's worth noting that", "It's important to note that", "It goes without saying", "It cannot be overstated".

**Stale connectors:** moreover, furthermore, additionally, that said (overused), with that in mind, in conclusion, ultimately, essentially, fundamentally, on the other hand (overused).

**Setup-knockdown clichés:** "X isn't just Y, it's Z", "Not only X but also Y", "More than just X", "It's not about X. It's about Y." All of these are fine once. Twice in a post and the reader notices the pattern.

**Marketing verbs:** revolutionise, transform, empower, unlock potential, drive value, deliver impact, bring to life.

**False intimacy:** "Let's dive in", "Let's explore", "Let's unpack", "Let's break this down", "Buckle up".

If a banned phrase is genuinely the right word, that's vanishingly rare. Find the simpler one.

## Structural tells

**The rule of three.** AI loves triplets: "clear, concise, and compelling". Real writers use two, four, one. Vary it. If three of your last five paragraphs use a triplet, kill at least one.

**The tidy summary close.** AI almost always ends with a forward-looking, gently inspiring summary sentence. ("And that's the real opportunity.") Don't. End on the strongest specific image or line you have. If the post earns the close, write a line that earns it. If not, just stop.

**Symmetric sentence rhythm.** AI writes sentences of similar length back-to-back. Real writing varies wildly. A long, winding sentence with several clauses and turns. Then a short one. Then one more. Vary length on purpose.

**Headline-stack structure.** AI breaks everything into headed sections with bullet points underneath. davidgentile.net posts are short essays, not landing pages. One H2 per post is usually too many. Bullet points are for actual lists, not for breaking up prose.

**Hedging over commitment.** "This could potentially be one factor that may contribute..." Real writers commit. Cut every "could", "might", "potentially", "arguably" that isn't doing real epistemic work.

**Balance for its own sake.** AI feels obliged to present "both sides" even when the writer has a view. State the view. Acknowledge the counter once if it's strong. Move on.

## Tone tells

**The cheerful inevitability.** AI prose has no edges. Nobody is annoyed. Nothing is wrong. Everything is an exciting opportunity. davidgentile.net writing has a point of view. Annoyance, scepticism, dry humour, frustration are allowed and welcome.

**The over-explanation.** AI re-states the same idea three ways. ("In other words, what this means is, essentially...") Trust the reader. State it once, well.

**The apologetic disclaimer.** "Of course, this is just one perspective and reasonable people may disagree." Almost always cuttable.

**The polished conclusion that reaffirms the title.** AI loves to circle back to the framing it opened with. The reader already read the framing. Earn the close with a new specific or a sharp turn.

## Authenticity gives

You want the reader to feel a person was at the keyboard. Means:

- **Specific over generic.** "Daniel said on Tuesday" beats "stakeholders previously communicated". Names, days, numbers, products, weather, smells.
- **Show your taste.** "I think this is the wrong call because..." beats "Some have argued...". The reader came for your view.
- **Mid-thought.** Not every sentence has to be a complete idea. Fragments are fine. Trailing-offs are fine.
- **Conversational beats.** "Look." "Anyway." "Honestly." Used sparingly, these break the AI rhythm.
- **Concrete metaphors.** "Pays rent on your attention" beats "incurs cognitive overhead".
- **A small joke that doesn't try too hard.** One self-deprecating aside per post is fine.

## Length

A davidgentile.net post is **180-400 words** of body. Most should land closer to 220. If a draft is longer, you're probably over-explaining or hedging. Cut.

Sentences average 14-18 words but should range from 4 to 30. If every sentence is 18 words, you wrote AI prose.

## The review pass

Before declaring a draft done, do this:

1. **Em dash sweep.** Search the file for the character `—`. There should be zero. If there's one, replace it.
2. **Phrase blocklist sweep.** Search for the worst offenders (delve, leverage, robust, seamless, multifaceted, ultimately, in essence, that said). Each hit gets reviewed and almost always cut.
3. **Read it out loud.** Genuinely. If you stumble or it sounds like a press release, rewrite that sentence.
4. **Triplet check.** Count "X, Y, and Z" patterns. More than two in a 250-word post means cut at least one.
5. **The "did a person write this?" gut check.** If your gut says no, the reader's will too.

## When you are unsure

A writer with taste reads their own draft and goes "this sentence doesn't sound like me". Listen to that voice. Most of the work is just deletion.

If you can't tell whether something sounds AI, the safe bet is that it does. Cut it or rewrite it shorter.

## What this skill does NOT govern

- Code comments inside artefacts. Those are for engineering clarity, not voice.
- README files and skill files. Same. Although em dashes in those should still be avoided for consistency.
- Quoted material from other sources. Quote it as it is.

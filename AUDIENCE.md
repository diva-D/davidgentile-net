# Who davidgentile.net is for

I'm writing for myself. That's the honest answer, and it's also the strategy.

The reader I have in mind is someone like me: a technically literate operator who spends their day at the intersection of structure, systems, and data, and is now watching AI reshape all three at once. Not a developer specifically. Not a researcher. Not a productivity-porn consumer. Someone who builds and runs things, cares about leverage, and has the pattern-matcher to tell real mechanism from hype.

## What this reader already believes

- Systems and structure are how work scales.
- Most software is badly designed and most orgs hemorrhage attention.
- Abstractions matter. Mechanism matters more.
- AI is real and is going to change how work happens. The question isn't whether, it's what specifically.

## What this reader is skeptical of

- Thought leadership that doesn't describe a mechanism.
- "AI will transform X" claims with no concrete before/after.
- Productivity systems sold as a lifestyle.
- Any sentence that could have been written by an LLM trying to sound insightful.

## The subject

Starting point: the clash between structure / systems / data and AI, and what comes out the other side. Not AI news. Not tooling reviews. Not productivity tips. The specific moments where a structural assumption that held for decades stops holding, and what that means for how you build, run, or think about things.

That's where I'm starting, not where I'm stuck. The through-line isn't the topic — it's the treatment.

## The form

Short prose paired with a purpose-built interactive artefact. Publications like *The Pudding* are the reference — the kind of piece where you can *touch* the concept and feel the mechanism, not just read the author's summary of it.

This matters for two reasons. First, it's how I think best: a concept I can't build an interactive for is usually a concept I don't actually understand yet. Second, it's what makes a 300-word post worth reading in a world of infinite text — the interactive is the thing you can't get anywhere else.

Any topic that passes the "is there a real interactive you could build that makes the argument land" test is fair game, even if it sits outside the current cluster. The form is the commitment.

## What this implies for distribution

The reader lives on X, in specific newsletters, occasionally on LinkedIn, rarely on Reddit. Subreddit strategy should be considered a long tail, not a primary channel. The primary lift is: will a smart person who already follows me or finds me through a shared link get the argument, feel the mechanism, and want more?

## How to use this file

Read before drafting a post. Read again before writing social copy. If a draft doesn't fit the reader above, either the draft needs to change or the reader does — but don't ship a post that's trying to serve a reader this file doesn't describe.

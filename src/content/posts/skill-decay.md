Every practice app has the same wrong model of what a skill is. It assumes mastery is a checkbox: once you've proven you can do the thing, the ✓ stays checked forever. A skill you proved you could do last April is marked as a skill you have today.

This is the lie. Real skills have *two* meters: one that ratchets up when you prove you can (mastery), and one that quietly drifts down when you don't practice (freshness). Play with the artefact below. Skip a day. Skip seven. Watch what happens to the bars when you try to keep them all green.

> A practice product that treats mastery and freshness as separate meters — and decides your daily session from the freshness map, not the mastery map — is a fundamentally different product than the ones that ship today. They have the wrong model of what a skill is.

Notice the tension: you have exactly one practice per day, and more skills than you can maintain. The question isn't *can I master this?* — it's *which skills do I let drift this week?*

The uncomfortable part: as you unlock more skills, you can't maintain them all. The system forces a choice about what to let go. That choice *is* the craft. It's also what no current app helps you make, because no current app has the right model of what's actually decaying.

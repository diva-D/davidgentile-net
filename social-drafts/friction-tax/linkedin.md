Every hour of unstructured work pays rent on your attention. You re-read the email. You dig Slack for what Daniel said Tuesday. You re-derive the next action from the raw pile, every time you look at it.

Structure stops the rent. GTD figured that out decades ago: a context, a status, and a next action on every item. But GTD has a second tax, which is why most people bounce off it. Someone still has to sit there and convert the pile into the schema.

AI removes the tax on producing structure. Not the need for it. Every org running on unstructured exhaust has been paying this tax forever. Until now it was the cost of work.

Now it's a choice.
